    Description

    Create a short 3D animation using Maya.

    Roles/Tools

    • Planning - Discord
    • Research - Google
    • Brainstorming/Storyboarding - Photoshop/Figma
    • 3D Animation - Maya
    • Video Editing - Premiere Pro

    About

    "Alien Jerry Springer" is a linear story based on popular American tabloid shows like Maury or Jerry Springer and references the game "Among Us" in the final scene. Instead of humans however, we featured Aliens as our cast of characters to emphasize unique and expressive reactions. The story takes place on a set much like the shows that inspired it, but with an alien theme applied. The plot follows an alien mother who came onto the show to try and prove that the alien assumed to be the father of her child is actually not its biological father in an attempt to stop paying him intergalactic child support. This animation was created over the course of two months in teams of five.

    Brainstorming/Research Phase

    We started this project in late September by researching for potential content we could base our animation around. We already agreed to make the characters in the animation aliens since we could create unique character designs rather than create character designs that already exist, so we just needed to decide what the story would be about. We brainstormed different ideas and shared clips of TV shows and found that comedic shows did a lot better in engaging us compared to other short videos. Thus, we decided to make our animation comedic to engage the viewers more. We discussed different comedy shows over Discord and narrowed our selections down to one, which was a skit done in the "Jerry Springer Show" where the host brings out an ex-couple and reveals whether or not the adults are the parents of the child through a DNA test. We based our story around this skit and swapped the human characters with aliens, and added a final scene where the fake parent gets ejected from the talk show into space, similar to the video game "Among Us."

    Once we had our overall idea figured out, we had to write a script. Since none of us were very good at writing a humorous script, we based most of our lines around the skit that inspired this idea and adjusted them so that they fit in with our video and was entertaining to watch.

    To get a PDF version of our script, click here.

    First Iteration: 2D Sketch Animation

    On the next stretch of our project, we needed to create an animatic that used 2D sketch as an extension of our team's storyboard. All of the scenes must match with the storyboard's and should have enought detail to see all the major key gestures and movements. To start this part of the project, we made a Figma file and then divided the work up so that everyone did an even amount of work, I worked on drawing all of the expressions for the father and mother, and my teammates worked on the other characters as well as the background. Once all of the drawings were done, my teammate animated them using Premiere Pro, and then sent the files to me so that I could add the subtitles to help the viewers understand what the characters were saying.

    Second Iteration: 3D Animation

    In the final weeks we took our animatic and started heading to the finish line in creating a fully animated video. We split up our tasks amongst the group and started by modelling the characters and stage pieces / environment. The remainder of the time was spent creating textures, lighting, staging, rigging the models, and finally animating them. Once the animation was done, we rendered out our scenes bit by bit. Lastly, we put the renders in to Adobe Premier and added sound, subtitles, and title cards to wrap up our final video. For the modelling part of the project, I worked on the models of the father as well as the mother, and helped to adjust their rigging. I also did all of the lighting in the film as I had the most experience with light composition due to my photography and art experience, and I assisted with staging the father in the back room as well as the camera transitions to that scene.

    Third Iteration: Recording, Rendering

    Once the animation was complete, we had to render it out and then combine it in Premiere Pro. Since my laptop GPU is not as good compared to my teammates, they rendered it and then I helped to combine it together. Unfortunately, we underestimated the amount of time it would take to render the whole project. After we finished the animation, we had three days left to render it. Once my teammate Dro started rendering it on his desktop, we realized that rendering the whole thing would take way longer than the time we had left, so we had to improvise. We decided to split up the rendering and lower the quality to finish it on time. After it was done rendering, I combined and edited the final few scenes and my teammates did the rest. Once everything was finished, we combined all the scenes together and then uploaded it to Youtube.

    Final Thoughts

    Overall, I am quite satisfied with our final result. Since none of us had much experience with working with Maya and creating 3D animations, this was a relatively new experience for us, and we managed to pull though at the end. However, if we had more time to do the project, we would definitely use it to properly render all the scenes in high quality.
