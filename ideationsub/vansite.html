    Description

    Design a web page for the city of Vancouver that informs the general public about a social issue in BC.

    Roles/Tools

    • Research - Google
    • Wireframes - Axure
    • Prototyping/Web Creation - Visual Studio Code

    About

    This project was to design a website that educated people about an issue that is affecting many people in downtown Vancouver. This website must have at least two subsections that influence the issue we are talking about, and must be made in HTML and CSS.

    Brainstorming/Research Phase

    To begin, we started to do research on societal issues that are present in BC using Google. There were many options to choose from such as mental health issues, violence against women, racial discrimination, and much more.

    After a discussion, we chose to do our project on the high cost of living in downtown Vancouver. We chose this issue because the high cost of living influences many other societal issues in the current world, such as homelessness and poverty. Additionally, there are many things that influence the high cost of living in Vancouver, such as rent and food prices, so there was ample information to do our project on.

    After finalizing our idea, we brainstormed how to organize our information in an appealing and coherent manner by planning out our website design.

    First Iteration: Thumbnail Sketches

    Once our information was organized, we made mini thumbnail sketches of possible website layouts. These website layouts were used to plan out how we could organize our information in a well-designed way and make it clear and easy to use.

    Second Iteration: Wireframes

    Once we made a few layouts, we chose the most visually appealing ones and started our wireframes. We created the wireframes using Axure and gott feedback over the weekend from our professor and TA. Once we were satisfied with our final product, we finalized the design and moved on to creating the website.

    Below are examples of wireframes done during the ideation phase of our project.

    Third Iteration: HTML/CSS, Publishing Website

    Once we finished the wireframes, we began coding the website from scratch. We split the work evenly with each person coding a few pages of the website. Once we had the content in, we designed the headers as well as the buttons that would link to each page. Once everything and designed and working, I sent my files to my teammate, who combined all of the pages and made sure that there were no broken or missing links. She then transferred the website onto our SFU domain and published it there.
