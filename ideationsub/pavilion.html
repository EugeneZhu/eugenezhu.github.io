    • Walkway Pavilion

    Team Members

    Eugene Zhu
    Candy Song
    Tianqi Wang

    Description

    Design a walkway pavilion that parametrically changes in Rhino and Grasshopper.

    Roles/Tools

    • Planning - Discord
    • Research - Google
    • Brainstorming - Photoshop
    • 3D Modelling - Rhino/Grasshopper

    About

    This project, done over the course of a month, required us to create a walkway pavilion that parametrically changes using Rhino and Grasshopper. This pavilion would be built in Holland Park in Surrey and would cover an 8 m x 12 m area at or around one of the intersections of the paths in the park. People must be able to use the pavilion when passing through the park, be able to use it to sit and relax with friends, and be able to use it to enjoy the view.

    Brainstorming/Research Phase

    We started this project by researching other pieces of architecture that we could use to inspire our own pavilion. From the pieces of architecture we examined, we chose one to do an in-depth analysis of the design and structure. The piece we chose was the Crane Pavilion at Crane Nature Preserve in Xianning, China. The images below are the sketches and writing we did about the pavilion.

    Once we finished our research, we moved onto brainstorming ideas about our own structure. We needed to make sure our pavilion looked good and also fits all of the criteria required for the structure. The requirements include:

    • The pavilion will provide a clear spatial definitions following the spatial design principles.
    • The spatial composition should allow people pass through under the pavilion, sit alone or with friends for socializing, or enjoy the view in a sunny day.
    • There will be an abstract sculpture (60 w x 60 l x 240 h) to be exhibited under the pavilion's canopy. Your design should provide a dedicated space for this sculpture.
    • The spatial design should provide continuity in circulation in the park while providing a pleasant experience.
    • The pavilion design should integrate the canopy structure and the structure that will support it.

    We made a bunch of sketches on paper and then discussed as a group which ones looked good enough to be used for our final project. We ended up choosing two of our best designs and then refining them to present it to our classmates and professor during the next class.

    First Iteration: Pavilion Adjustments, Final Sketch

    After presenting it to the class and getting feedback, we realized that the designs did not fit all of the requirements. Thus, we chose to work on the first design, which was simpler, and adjusted and changed it so that it fit all of the criteria. We discussed how to change it over Discord and then my teammate sketched out the final design on paper. We included a multiview with dimensions of the pavilion as well as a bird's eye view of where the structure would be located and how it would look from afar.

    Second Iteration: Final CAD Pavilion, Poster

    Once we had this step of our project done, all we had to do now was to make the digital model on Rhino and Grasshopper. My teammates and I worked on the structure together by sending the file back and forth between each other. We also needed to make a video showcasing how our model parametrically changes when adjusting it as well as a poster that showcases our 3D parametric model of the pavilion. Thus, while one of us were busy with working on the model, the others were working on the other stuff. We split the work as evenly as possible over the last stretch of the project.
